Keynote Talk 1 (Thursday, August 24th)

Experiences with Learned Systems: The good, The Bad and The Ugly

Speaker: Haibo Chen

Distinguished Professor, Shanghai Jaio Tong University



Talk abstract

Many problems in systems software can be transformed into those using machine learning. In this talk, I will share our past experiences of defining and optimizing optimization problems in systems software using machine learning, resulting in a set of learned systems ranging from indexes, concurrency control, and distributed key/value stores. Based on these, I will further describe the learned lessons in applying machine learnings for systems, and discuss possible challenges and opportunities in fostering better synergy between systems and machine learning.

Bio

Haibo Chen is a Distinguished Professor of Shanghai Jiao Tong University, where he directs the Institute for Parallel and Distributed Systems (IPADS). His main research areas are operating systems and distributed systems. He received Best Paper Awards from ASPLOS, EuroSys and VEE, Test of Time Award from DSN, Best Paper Honorable Mention and Research Highlight Award from SIGMOD, Honorable Mention of The Dennis M. Ritchie Thesis Award (Advisor) from SIGOPS. He currently chairs ACM SIGOPS, serves on the editorial board member and co-chair of Special Sections of Communications of the ACM, Program Committee of SOSP 2023/OSDI 2024, PC co-chair of EuroSys 2025, and the inaugural technical steering committee chair of OpenHarmony, an open-source operating system deployed on hundreds of millions of devices. He is an IEEE Fellow and an ACM Distinguished Member.



Keynote Talk 2 (Friday, August 25th)

Behind the scenes of SmartThings

Speaker: Jaeyeon Jung

Executive Vice President and Head of SmartThings, Samsung Electronics



Talk abstract

To process over 150K events per second to support 120M+ connected devices to the SmartThings platform from 280M+ registered users globally, my team has built and been operating a cloud-based system composed of 200+ microservices deployed across 4 regions. We also support various connectivity protocols like Zigbee, ZWave, Thread, and Wi-Fi through SmartThings Hub to integrate many such partner devices as doorlocks, light bulbs, motion sensors, smart plugs, and etc. In this talk, I’ll share several insights that I learned by working with many talented engineers in my team while building a scalable, secure, performant, and extensible smart home platform.

Bio

Jaeyeon Jung is Executive Vice President and since 2020 Head of the SmartThings Team at Samsung Electronics. She oversees the engineering and the strategy of the Samsung’s IoT platform, which includes SmartThings applications on mobile, TV, appliances, watch, and PC; the cloud-based backend that connects hundreds of millions of devices; and embedded software for device connectivity. Her team also creates and operates smart solutions like SmartThings Find to help users locate misplaced ever-growing Galaxy products such as smartphones, tablets, laptops, earbuds, and watches along with their precious belongings with Galaxy SmartTags. Prior to joining Samsung in 2016, she was with Microsoft Research in Redmond (2011-2016) and Intel Research Seattle (2007-2011), where she led a number of research projects focused around the security and privacy in smart homes and mobile devices. She obtained her PhD from MIT in 2006.
